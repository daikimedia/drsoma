What Is Breast Reduction?

Breast reduction, or reduction mammaplasty, is a surgical procedure that reduces breast size by removing excess breast tissue, fat, and stretched skin, while reshaping and lifting the breast for a natural appearance.

It is both a functional and aesthetic procedure. The goal is not simply to "make breasts smaller" but to achieve a balanced size, improved symmetry, and a healthier nipple position.

Types of Breast Reduction

Technique selection depends on breast size, degree of sagging, and desired final proportion.

1

Standard Reduction

Reduction + Lift

Most common. Removes tissue and excess skin while lifting the breast. Repositions the nipple to a natural height for a lighter, balanced contour.

2

Large-Volume Reduction

Functional Focus

For patients with significant heaviness and discomfort. Involves greater tissue removal and stronger internal support. Prioritizes daily comfort.

3

With Areola Reshaping

Detail Refinement

For patients with stretched, enlarged areolas. Reduces areola diameter to match the new breast size, enhancing overall symmetry and aesthetic.

Post-Operative Recovery

Immediate / Week 1

Support bra applied. Mild to moderate soreness. Walking encouraged immediately. Rest and avoid lifting.

Week 2–3

Return to desk work for many. Bruising improves. Light walking continues. Support bra essential.

Week 4–6

Resume light exercise with approval. Improved posture and comfort noticeable. Shape continues settling.

Month 3–6

Final contour becomes natural. Scars soften and fade. Long-term comfort benefits clear.

Who Is a Good Candidate?

  • Large or heavy breasts causing discomfort.
  • Chronic back, neck, or shoulder strain.
  • Skin irritation or rashes under breasts.
  • Stable body weight.
  • Non-smoker or willing to stop temporarily.
Not suitable if: Pregnant/breastfeeding, uncontrolled medical conditions, planning major weight loss, or smoking without willingness to stop.
