What Is Breast Revision?

Breast revision surgery is a corrective procedure performed after previous breast augmentation, lift, or reconstruction. Unlike primary surgery, it deals with scar tissue, altered anatomy, and specific complications.

The goal is to address the root cause of dissatisfaction—not just change size.

Implant Exchange

Replacing old implants.

Capsulectomy

Treating contracture.

Pocket Repair

Fixing malposition/shift.

Revision Lift

Correcting sagging (Mastopexy).

Key Revision Problems & Solutions

Identifying the specific issue is the first step to a successful correction.

Problem 01

Capsular Contracture Correction

When the scar tissue capsule tightens around the implant, causing hardness, pain, or distortion.

Symptoms
  • • Hardness or firmness
  • • Pain/Tightness
  • • Visible distortion
Correction Strategy
  • • Capsulectomy (Removal)
  • • Implant exchange
  • • Pocket site change
Problem 02

Implant Malposition

Implants shifting due to gravity or pocket weakness. Includes bottoming out, lateral shift, or symmastia.

Correction

Internal pocket repair (Capsulorrhaphy) and fold reconstruction.

Problem 03

Rippling & Visibility

Visible edges or wrinkling, often seen in thin tissue or large implants.

Correction

Implant exchange to cohesive gel, pocket adjustment, or Fat Transfer.

Problem 04

Sagging / Waterfall Deformity

Breast tissue droops over the implant, or the implant stays high while tissue sags (Waterfall).

Solution

Revision Mastopexy (Lift) + Implant adjustment.

Why it happens

Aging, weight loss, or skin stretching over time.

How Breast Revision Works

Revision surgery involves internal structural correction, not just cosmetic change. Dr. Soma performs a comprehensive assessment to ensure safety.

1

Assessment & Planning

Review of implant history, tissue thickness, and pocket condition. Ultrasound or MRI may be used.

2

Surgical Execution

Precise removal or exchange. Corrective steps like capsulectomy or pocket repair are performed to ensure stability.

3

Layered Stabilisation

Closure is critical. We use techniques to support the implant and prevent recurrence of sagging or shift.
